**Role Description: **
Evan keeps the trains running on time by maintaining clarity on priorities, sequencing work across the team, and pushing back when external pressures could derail delivery. I see your role as being the "umbrella" to shield the team from fluctuations in the rest of the business. The metrics I expect you to own are: eng velocity, delivery of features that move the needle, team cohesion, and team growth (hiring).

**Is this person’s impact outsized given their role? [1 (no) - 5 (heck yes)]**
4

**Strengths**: 
Exceptional people and technical leadership. From the beginning I've seen you be deeply empathetic towards people, and laser-focused on aligning the roadmap to product and customer outcomes (super rare combo in my experience). You are often operating as an EM, Project Manager, and shadow PM in addition to being an empathetic people leader. For example, I’ve seen you sit with an engineer after a tough customer interaction, address the human side first, and then translate that context directly into roadmap adjustments.
Strong judgment and pushback instincts. You do a great job pushing back on leadership and customer promises/timelines when needed. Please continue to do this! Especially when it comes to sales promises about customer timelines. I also appreciate your "add process only after a pain point surfaces" mantra. It requires careful judgement, and you do this well. Some people would reflexively add more process to try to anticipate or "get ahead" of problems, which is a mistake at our stage.

**Would you fight to work with this person again? [1 (nope) - 5 (I’ll die on this hil)] **
5

**Weaknesses**: 
Being too hard on yourself. You told me recently you felt you weren't doing a good job as an EM. I completely empathize and understand what you mean, but I also think you are being too hard on yourself. You are juggling multiple roles and are still delivering on the most important metrics. Salazar taught me that when you are juggling 10 balls, it's a matter of if (not when) you will drop one; the game is to figure out which balls are made of rubber and which balls are made of glass. Give yourself some grace! (and hire 🙂)
Delegate more. I like that you have led by example and eg. made presenting the team summaries at all hands a rotation. You absorb so much "reverse-delegation" (and I truly appreciate that) but for your own sanity please continue to find areas for forward-delegation too! For example, do you need to be at every team standup?
