# Slack Norms & Communication Guidelines
**Last updated: January 2026** **Owner: People Operations** **Public URL:** https://docs.google.com/document/d/1IicF6BDjKYCVNO4yjH2KdliuEmZHkChQeYmbtSvXq4s/edit?tab=t.0#heading=h.pvasortl2pgj  **Status: CURRENT**
## Why This Matters
Slack is our primary communication tool. With 200+ employees across multiple time zones, clear norms help everyone stay informed without drowning in noise.
## Channel Naming Conventions
- **#team-[name]**: Team-specific channels (e.g., #team-engineering, #team-marketing, #team-sales).
- **#proj-[name]**: Project-specific channels (e.g., #proj-redesign, #proj-q1-launch).
- **#ask-[team]**: Cross-team questions (e.g., #ask-engineering, #ask-legal, #ask-finance).
- **#social-[topic]**: Non-work interests (e.g., #social-dogs, #social-gaming, #social-cooking).
## Required Channels
All employees must join:

- **#general**: Company-wide announcements and updates. Keep chatter to a minimum.
- **#announcements**: Leadership and People Ops updates. Only admins can post.
- **#new-hires**: Introduce yourself here on Day 1! Share your name, role, team, location, and a fun fact.
- **Your team channel** (e.g., #team-engineering)
## Communication Norms
- **Threads**: Always reply in threads. Do not reply in the main channel body.
- **@here and @channel**: Use sparingly. @here is for time-sensitive items relevant to people currently online. @channel is for urgent, everyone-must-see messages. When in doubt, do not use either.
- **DMs vs. channels**: Default to channels. DMs are for private/sensitive matters only. If a DM thread becomes a decision, move it to a channel.
- **Response time**: During business hours (9am-6pm local), aim to respond within 4 hours. If you need something faster, say so explicitly.
- **Status**: Keep your Slack status updated. Use "In a meeting," "OOO," "Focus time," etc. If you are out for the day, set it.
## Emoji Conventions
- Checkmark: "Done" or "Acknowledged"
- Eyes: "Looking into it"
- Thumbs up: "Sounds good" / general agreement
- Raising hand: "I can help" or "I'll take this"
## Bots & Integrations
- Jira, GitHub, CircleCI, PagerDuty, and Datadog integrations post to relevant channels. Do not mute these in incident-related channels.
- Standup bot runs daily in team channels. Fill it in by 10am local time.
## New Hire Introductions
On your first day, post an introduction in #new-hires. Here is a template:

Hi everyone! I'm [Name], joining as [Role] on the [Team] team, based in [Location]. Before Acme, I was at [Previous Company/School]. Fun fact: [Something interesting about you]. Excited to be here!
## Manager Welcome Messages
When a new team member joins, HR is responsible for posting a welcome message in #general before or on the employee's first day. The message should include the employee's name, role, team, location, previous company, and a callout to their manager. Keep it warm and brief -- a few sentences is enough.

Example:

Excited to welcome [Name] to the [Team] team! They're joining as [Role], based in [Location], and come to us from [Previous Company]. They'll be reporting to [Manager Name] -- say hello in #new-hires and give them a warm welcome! 🎉
