1. **Strengths**: 
Can-do attitude. I've seen you jump into ownership from the very beginning and quickly and take on thorny, complex tasks without complaint. I really appreciate your low-ego, "let's fix it" mentality. I also love that you are very willing to jump in to help anyone else on the team, no matter if their problem is related to your work or not. 1. **Would you fight to work with this person again? [1 (nope) - 5 (I’ll die on this hil)] **
  1. 2 - I expect this to grow as you increase your impact.
1. **Weaknesses**: 
Self-review code first before peer review. I've observed that you don't always thoroughly review your own code before you ask someone else to review it. This is the "other side of the coin" of agentic coding tools - without careful review, sloppy AI code can sneak in. Concretely, I’d expect you to run and sanity-check your changes locally, re-read the diff end-to-end, and leave notes in the PR about anything you’re unsure about before asking for review.
Attention to detail. I've experienced a frustration in the code review process with you a few times: I flag something or we discuss a change, but I don't see the feedback acted upon when I return for the follow-up review. As a reviewer who needs to context switch a lot, it's hard to remember exactly what we discussed on each PR, and I expect the PR author to diligently follow up on each item that was discussed or commented on. Focusing on careful attention to detail in your PRs will reduce the number of time-consuming re-reviews that are needed. One way to do this would be explicitly replying to each comment or checklist item in the PR, so reviewers can quickly verify what changed.

  1. 4 - I expect this number to grow as you increase your impact in the company.
